About Nikolajs

Who I am, what I do, and what I want to do.

Nikolajs Veidis is a second year student at Muhlenberg College who is focusing his studies in finance and computer science. He is bilingual in Latvian and English and somewhat proficient in Russian. Nikolajs remains involved in the American Latvian Youth Association and is a graduate from Garezers, a Latvian summer high school. He is proficient in Java, Python, and HTML/CSS and is learning Ruby on Rails. Outside of school, Nikolajs is an avid traveler and is constantly seeking adventure.

Latvian startup experience

This past summer Nikolajs was working in Riga, Latvia at a small software based startup called Funderful. He was placed into a true startup atmosphere where he learned many great new things. With an American background, which no other team members had, he was put to task to locate possible future contacts. The thousands of new potential clients were placed into a spreadsheet with all of their information. Nikolajs also pitched and developed a future campaign idea that was to be used in the American University Giving day campaign. The development of this campaign went futhure than gathering the idea, but actually creating a template website using the software, Balsamiq. All in all it was an amazing experience and Nikolajs was able to really see what the startup life was all about.

Coding background

Growing up Nikolajs had access to a computer, but choose not to use it. At a young age Nikolajs thrived on th outdoor life. During the warmer days of the summer and spring Nikolajs's mother had to bring him breakfast, lunch, and dinner outside because he would always be outside. At about the age of eight Nikolajs started playing computer games with friends in the times where it was too cold to go outside. Since other consoles weren't present in his house, the computer was where he was able to explore the video game world. Nikolajs always knew about the concepts of computer programming, but didn't actually start to code until his third year of high school. He enrolled in a Computer Science I class which was used Java. After Computer Science I and II he got into the Advanced Placement course in his high school. The majority of the time spent in these courses was listening to lectures, which seemed ineffective, but the times where programming took place, Nikolajs loved every second of it. Outside of classes Nikolajs used his knowledge of Java in programming old video games that he used to play. Acquiring the source code of the games he would add his own code and recreate the game to his pleasing. This was all done for pure pleasure and no profit was made.

In the first semester of his freshman year at Muhlenberg College Nikolajs enrolled in the Computer Science II class. The class was also based in Java and had a split lecture/coding type of atmosphere. Nikolajs was also introduced to different types of algorithms that could help him in future programming.
